# Discovery Questions — Top 5 Partners (Mom-Test compliant)

**Mom-Test rules applied (Rob Fitzpatrick) — strictly:**
- Talk about their life, not our idea.
- Ask about specific past behaviour, not hypothetical future behaviour.
- Listen for problems, not compliments.
- Dig into the specifics of the last time it happened.
- Get a concrete commitment or escalation, not a hypothetical.

This document is a call checklist. It is NOT a deck. Do not pitch PerSense/JITAI/the dashboard until the buyer has named their pain in their own words.

---

### PA-001 — CUF Senologia (Centro de Senologia, CUF Descobertas / CUF Tejo)
**Goal of this conversation**: Validate that the senology director (or the head of CUF Oncologia operations) owns survivorship as a quality/NPS metric, that the <20% psych-support figure [EV-016] matches their lived reality, and that a sub-EUR-20K-ACV SaaS contract can be signed at unit level (not blocked at José de Mello Saúde central procurement) within ~6 weeks.
**Buyer persona**: Head of CUF Senologia / Director de Oncologia at the unit level; secondary: CUF Quality Director.

**Open with current state (not our pitch)**
1. Walk me through the follow-up pathway you currently run for a BCS patient in months 0–24 after active treatment — who does she see, when, and how do you know between visits whether she is doing OK?
2. Of the BCS patients you followed last quarter, how many were referred to formal psychological support? How do you track that number today?

**Probe the pain (concrete past behaviour)**
3. The last time a survivor came back distressed months after treatment — fatigue, anxiety, scan-anxiety — what did your team actually do, step by step, and how long did the path-to-help take?
4. When was the last time the survivorship gap came up in your quality / accreditation report, or in a conversation with CUF central oncology direction? What was decided?

**Test commitment, not interest**
5. Who else inside CUF — central oncology direction, DPO, finance — would need to be in the room for an 8–12-week survivorship pilot at this unit to actually start?
6. Is there a current quality-improvement budget line, an accreditation cycle, or a 2026 plan that an PoV at EUR 800–1500/month for one site could fit into without a new approval round?

**Escalation ask**
7. If the timeline and the alert-volume budget look right to you, who is the right person to introduce me to next — and would you be willing to send the intro this week?

**Anti-patterns to avoid in this conversation**
- "Would you use a dashboard that…?"
- "Do you think AI for survivorship is a good idea?"
- Pitching the JITAI / sensing mechanism before the head has named the <20% gap as their problem.
- Quoting the EUR 800–1500 price before they have asked.

### PA-002 — Lusíadas Centro Mama (Lusíadas Lisboa / Porto)
**Goal of this conversation**: Validate that the Centro Mama clinical lead owns the patient-experience narrative Lusíadas markets externally, that the survivorship gap is a real reputational/clinical pain (not just a marketing line), and that a single-site PoV is signable inside Lusíadas medical direction without a multi-month group procurement.
**Buyer persona**: Centro Mama clinical lead / coordinator de senologia; secondary: Lusíadas Director Clínico.

**Open with current state (not our pitch)**
1. How is the Centro Mama follow-up pathway structured today — who is the named person responsible for survivorship, and how do you measure whether it is working?
2. The Centro Mama is publicly positioned as integrated multidisciplinary care. In the last 12 months, what did integration look like specifically for psychological support of BCS patients between consultations?

**Probe the pain (concrete past behaviour)**
3. Tell me about the most recent BCS patient who reached out between scheduled consultations. What channel did she use, and how was that handled?
4. When was the last patient-experience or NPS issue you saw that was traceable back to the survivorship phase rather than to active treatment?

**Test commitment, not interest**
5. If we ran an 8–12-week PoV at Centro Mama with ~30 patients and a bounded alert-volume budget per clinician, who internally needs to sign — clinical lead, medical direction, DPO, finance — and what is each one's typical turnaround?
6. Is there a recent or planned Lusíadas patient-experience initiative or quality-report cycle where a survivorship metric (alerts caught, time-to-intervention, PHQ-9/GAD-7 trajectory) would land?

**Escalation ask**
7. If this fits, who is the right next person to meet — the Director Clínico, the DPO, or the marketing/quality lead — and can you forward this conversation summary to them?

**Anti-patterns to avoid in this conversation**
- "Would you be interested in a digital survivorship platform?"
- "How big could the contract be?" (price talk before pain).
- Pitching CUF or Luz comparables before Lusíadas has named their own gap (avoid framing this as "everyone is doing it").

### PA-003 — Hospital da Luz Centro Clínico da Mama (Luz Saúde / Fidelidade)
**Goal of this conversation**: Validate that the Centro Clínico da Mama clinical director or Luz Saúde innovation team is open to a PoV at a single Luz site, identify whether decision rights sit at hospital level or at Luz Saúde central, and probe whether the Fidelidade insurer linkage opens any co-funding / shared-data conversation that bridges to BT-05.
**Buyer persona**: Centro Clínico da Mama clinical director; secondary: Luz Saúde Innovation lead; tertiary: Fidelidade health-product stakeholder.

**Open with current state (not our pitch)**
1. How is BCS follow-up structured at the Centro Clínico da Mama today — what is the standard cadence, and who carries responsibility for emotional/psychological care between visits?
2. Luz Saúde markets digital innovation publicly. Of the last two or three digital-health pilots you have run at the breast unit, what worked clinically and what stalled?

**Probe the pain (concrete past behaviour)**
3. Walk me through the last time a BCS patient escalated between consultations — what was the actual sequence, and what did your team wish you had known earlier?
4. When was the last time survivorship outcomes came up in a conversation with Luz Saúde central direction or with Fidelidade on the insurer side?

**Test commitment, not interest**
5. For a single-site 8–12-week PoV at EUR 800–1500/month with a contracted alert-volume cap per clinician, who is the smallest internal coalition that can say yes — hospital director only, or does it require Luz Saúde central innovation?
6. Is there a current Luz Saúde innovation cycle, a Fidelidade health-product roadmap, or a 2026 patient-experience initiative this PoV could plug into?

**Escalation ask**
7. If you would like to take this further, can you connect me directly to the right counterpart at Luz Saúde innovation or Fidelidade health products this week?

**Anti-patterns to avoid in this conversation**
- "Would Fidelidade pay for this?" (jumping to BT-05 before BT-03 evidence exists).
- Praising Luz Saúde's innovation posture (compliments are not data).
- Pitching the dashboard before the clinical director has named the between-consultation gap.

### PA-004 — Sociedade Portuguesa de Senologia (SPS)
**Goal of this conversation**: Validate whether SPS would endorse a between-consultation BCS detection PoV protocol, whether the psico-oncologia working group is the right route (versus the main board), and whether the current SPS leadership can introduce us to the senology heads at CUF / Lusíadas / Luz inside the next 4 weeks.
**Buyer persona**: SPS President or psico-oncologia working group lead.

**Open with current state (not our pitch)**
1. How does SPS currently view the gap between BCS clinical follow-up and emotional/psychological follow-up in PT senology centres — what does the field already agree on, and what is still contested?
2. In the last 12–24 months, what has the psico-oncologia working group actually produced or recommended on this gap, and how have member centres responded?

**Probe the pain (concrete past behaviour)**
3. Tell me about the last time a member raised a concrete proposal at a working group on between-consultation care — what was the proposal, and what blocked it?
4. When was the last SPS congress topic that members said "we still do not know how to solve this in practice"?

**Test commitment, not interest**
5. If we ran a PoV with one private senology centre and shared anonymised aggregate results with the working group, what would SPS need to see in the protocol to consider an endorsement letter?
6. Is there a 2026 congress slot, a working-group meeting, or a position paper window where week-12 results could be presented — and what is the timeline?

**Escalation ask**
7. Could you introduce me to the senology head at one of CUF / Lusíadas / Luz this week, even if SPS endorsement comes later?

**Anti-patterns to avoid in this conversation**
- "Would SPS endorse this?" (yes/no hypothetical — useless data).
- Pitching PerSense's AUC 0.78 number before SPS has spoken about the gap in their own terms.
- Treating SPS as a buyer — they are an endorser/connector, never a customer.

### PA-005 — IPO Lisboa Francisco Gentil — Psico-oncologia & Sobrevivência
**Goal of this conversation**: Validate whether IPO Lisboa Psico-Oncologia would act as a backup PoV research-site if the private channel does not convert by week 4, surface the realistic ethics-committee timeline (so the team can decide by week 4 whether IPO is achievable inside the 8–12-week PoV window), and identify a co-investigator who anchors the BT-04 EU-grant expansion.
**Buyer persona**: Head of IPO Lisboa Psico-Oncologia / Programa de Sobrevivência coordinator.

**Open with current state (not our pitch)**
1. How is the BCS survivorship pathway structured at IPO Lisboa today — who runs months 0–24 follow-up, and where do psycho-oncology referrals come from?
2. In the last year, what fraction of the BCS patients in active follow-up actually saw a psicólogo da equipa, and what blocked the others?

**Probe the pain (concrete past behaviour)**
3. Walk me through the most recent case where a survivor's emotional state deteriorated between scheduled consultations — what did the team know, when, and what did you do?
4. When was the last research-collaboration or pilot you ran on between-consultation digital monitoring — what worked, what didn't, and why?

**Test commitment, not interest**
5. If we proposed a research-collaboration PoV (no commercial contract, just co-investigator + co-authorship + AUC replication), what is the realistic ethics-committee timeline today, and is fast-track possible if pre-existing protocol elements apply?
6. Is there a current FCT, EU Mission Cancer, or internal IPO research-funding cycle where this PoV could fit as a preliminary study?

**Escalation ask**
7. If this is interesting, who in the IPO research office should I talk to about ethics timelines this week, and would you be a co-investigator on the protocol?

**Anti-patterns to avoid in this conversation**
- "Would IPO buy a SaaS subscription?" (IPO is not a BT-03 buyer — research site only).
- Pitching commercial pricing or hospital-group expansion plans.
- Treating the AUC 0.78 internal number as a finished result rather than as a hypothesis to replicate on a fresh cohort.
