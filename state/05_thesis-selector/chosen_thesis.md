# Chosen Business Thesis: BT-03 — PT private oncology white-label (CUF / Lusiadas / Luz Saude)

## One-line
Subscription dashboard + JITAI sensing layer for BCS senology follow-up, sold per-hospital to PT private oncology operators that already differentiate on patient experience.

## Who pays / who uses
- Customer: PT private hospital groups — head of oncology / senology / quality at CUF Oncologia, Lusiadas, Luz Saude.
- Buyer/User split: Hospital pays SaaS; senology team uses the dashboard; BCS patient uses the app; oncologist remains the accountable physician.

## Revenue model
- Mechanism: SaaS per-hospital, monthly subscription + light setup; optional per-active-case overage.
- Price point hypothesis: EUR 800–1500/hospital/month base (anchor: EV-084 PerSense Phase-2 EUR 500–2K/mo; EV-083 white-label B2B EUR 15–50K/yr; EV-047 PoV threshold ≥EUR 200/mo).
- Unit economics implication: EUR 10–18K ACV/hospital, dwarfed by the alternative spend (~EUR 60–80K/yr for a psycho-oncologist FTE the head cannot hire); gross margin SaaS-typical once 3+ sites share the platform.

## TAM (serviceable)
EUR 0.5–1.0M/yr PT serviceable. ~3 large groups × ~8 senology-active sites = ~24 hospital-units; capture 30–50% over 3 yr = 7–12 active sites @ EUR 800–1500/mo → EUR 67K–216K/yr Yr2; full PT private TAM EUR 0.5–1M/yr including overages and module expansion. Sanity ceiling: PT digital-mental-health market EUR 40–60M (EV-074). Anchors: EV-074, EV-083, EV-084, EV-047, EV-019.

## Time to first revenue
6 months. Bottlenecks: (1) procurement cycle inside CUF/Lusiadas/Luz (no signed LOI yet); (2) clinician-pathway integration so dashboards are actually used (Limbic-style referral integration, EV-026); (3) BCS month-0–24 follow-up standardisation across the three groups.

## Regulatory path
MDR Class I (wellness / triage decision-support, no diagnostic claim) initially; upgrade to Class IIa once revenue funds it. Cost: <EUR 30K initial (Class I self-cert + GDPR); IIa upgrade EUR 300–700K (EV-057). Timeline: Class I 3–6 mo; IIa 24–36 mo (EV-058). GDPR liability precedent EUR 2.3M (EV-062).

## What this thesis demands of the PoV pilot
This section drives `pilot-designer`'s success criteria. The 8–12-week PoV must produce, no exceptions:

- **≥2 PT private senology units sign LOI or paid pilot at ≥EUR 200/clinician-or-hospital/month** by week 12 (EV-047) — without this, the SaaS hypothesis is unfalsified and BT-03 collapses.
- **Clinician burden quantified and bounded**: alerts/clinician/week generated by the JITAI layer, with an explicit upper bound the senology team confirms it can absorb without new FTE hires (problem.md v2 open issue 3). If burden > absorbable, the pilot has failed regardless of detection performance.
- **Between-consultation detection precision/recall reported** on the BCS months-0–24 cohort, with PHQ-9/GAD-7 deltas at week 0/4/8/12 — this is the clinical credibility floor the buyer's quality director will demand and is the asset BT-01 (DiGA) and BT-02 (pharma) inherit.
- **Patient consent + activation ≥50%** of eligible BCS referred from senology consultation, materially lifting the 34% iNNOVSensing baseline (EV-041); shows the clinician-mediated referral pathway works.
- **Engagement ≥60% weekly active among consented patients across 8+ weeks** (anchor: Limbic 33% NHS adoption, EV-026) so dashboards are not free-rider artefacts.
- **Buyer-facing dashboard shipped as the deliverable**, not a slide deck: NPS-relevant metrics (alerts caught vs missed, time-to-intervention, PHQ-9/GAD-7 trajectory) usable in the hospital's quality / accreditation reporting.
- **Deployment cost ≤EUR 150K total** to validate (EV-080: PT validation 50% cheaper than NHS) — protects capital efficiency claim.
- **Data ownership + GDPR DPIA executed and signed** with at least one hospital DPO, since EV-062 EUR 2.3M GDPR precedent makes a breach existential.

## Top risks
- PT private market concentration (3 groups) — losing one buyer halves the TAM.
- Free-rider dashboards: adopted but unused; engagement collapses without referral-pathway integration (EV-026).
- Regulatory drift: as evidence claims expand, Class IIa becomes mandatory and EUR 300–700K capex returns (EV-057).

## Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder) — for the BUYER, not the end-user

Buyer = head of senology / oncology operations / quality at a PT private hospital group. Not the BCS patient.

**Customer Profile (the buyer's world)**
- **Jobs-to-be-Done**:
  - J1: Deliver differentiated BCS survivorship care that protects retention/NPS vs SNS, on a fixed FTE budget, while meeting accreditation/quality reporting obligations.
  - J2: Surface and intervene on between-consultation deterioration without expanding the senology team or escalating to unscheduled care.
- **Pains** (each tied to a number):
  - P1: <20% of their BCS receive formal psych support today (EV-016) → an undetected affective relapse becomes a churned patient + complaint, directly visible in NPS and quality reporting.
  - P2: A psycho-oncologist FTE costs ~EUR 60–80K/yr — not approvable in current budget envelope, yet 30–40% of BCS have clinical anxiety/depression (EV-019).
  - P3: Clinician burden / alert overload — senology team has no headroom for unbounded alerts; iNNOVSensing baseline consent at only 34% (EV-041) shows current tooling does not earn its keep.
- **Gains** (measurable, role-specific):
  - G1: NPS / accreditation-visible survivorship metric the head can put in the quality report (alerts caught, time-to-intervention, PHQ-9/GAD-7 trajectory).
  - G2: Earlier detection of affective relapse → fewer unscheduled encounters and fewer downstream complaints.
  - G3: A second revenue/data line (pharma research-services BT-02, EU grant co-applicant BT-04) running on top of the same deployment, at zero marginal effort to the buyer's team.

**Value Map (what we offer)**
- **Products & Services** (buyer-facing, not the patient app):
  - PS1: Senology-team dashboard with prioritised alerts, PHQ-9/GAD-7 trajectories, and quality-report-ready exports.
  - PS2: JITAI sensing + EMA layer running on the BCS patient app, configured for the months-0–24 follow-up pathway.
  - PS3: GDPR DPIA + Class I self-certification pack, hospital-DPO co-signed, and a referral-pathway SOP integrated into the senology consultation workflow.
- **Pain Relievers** (1:1 mapping to Pains):
  - PR1 → P1: Closes the <20% psych-support gap (EV-016) by turning the BCS app into a continuously-monitored survivorship channel; missed affective relapses become a measured, reported number.
  - PR2 → P2: SaaS at EUR 800–1500/hospital/month (EV-084) ≈ EUR 10–18K/yr — ~5× cheaper than a psycho-oncologist FTE, on opex not headcount.
  - PR3 → P3: Alert volume bounded and contracted as a pilot success criterion (≤X alerts/clinician/week, see PoV demands above); referral-pathway integration (Limbic-style, EV-026) prevents free-rider adoption.
- **Gain Creators** (1:1 mapping to Gains):
  - GC1 → G1: Quality-report-ready dashboard exports (PS1) directly populate the head's accreditation/NPS narrative.
  - GC2 → G2: AUC 0.78 BCS-specific detection (EV-043) on between-consultation signals → earlier intervention, fewer escalations.
  - GC3 → G3: Same deployment is the reference site for pharma research-services contracts (BT-02, EUR 100–300K/study) and EU grant consortia (BT-04) — the buyer becomes the academic anchor without extra effort.

VPC mapping is complete: every Pain has a Pain Reliever and every Gain has a Gain Creator. See Open Issues for items where a reliever exists but its magnitude is unproven.

## Open Issues
- **PR3 magnitude unproven**: the upper bound of "alerts/clinician/week the senology team can absorb" is not quantified (problem.md v2 open issue 3) — the PoV must produce this number or PR3 is aspirational.
- **PR2 conversion unproven**: no signed LOI from CUF/Lusiadas/Luz; the EUR 800–1500/mo price is anchored on EV-084/EV-083, not on a buyer signature.
- **GC2 generalisation**: PerSense AUC 0.78 (EV-043, internal_only, N=126) replication on a fresh PT cohort is the PoV's core scientific test; if it does not hold, GC2 weakens and BT-01/BT-02 inheritance is at risk.

## Why this thesis over the others (selection rationale)
BT-03 has the highest composite score (23) and the highest PoV alignment (5) — the existing 8–12-week PT senology pilot literally is this thesis, with no redesign required. It is also the fastest to first revenue (6 mo vs 9–30 mo for the alternatives) and capital-light (Class I, <EUR 30K), while still generating the BCS clinical evidence and proprietary data asset that unlock BT-01 (DiGA) and BT-02 (pharma) on expansion — satisfying the Trojan-Horse-with-expansion test.

## Alternatives kept on the bench (for the Evidence Notes appendix)
- BT-01 — DiGA companion-DTx for BCS survivorship (DE → EU) — composite 16; lost on time-to-revenue (30 mo) and capex (EUR 300–700K MDR IIa) without a DE clinical partner; remains the Yr3+ expansion play.
- BT-02 — Pharma companion / BCS adherence research-services — composite 19; lost on no named pharma counterparty and 6–12 mo procurement; will be layered on top of BT-03 deployments once a reference site exists.
- BT-04 — IPO / public oncology + EU-funded research consortium — composite 18; lost on lumpy non-recurring revenue, ~5.9% EIC success rate (EV-063), and absent PT public-DTx reimbursement pathway; becomes a parallel non-dilutive funding line.
- BT-05 — Insurer survivorship benefit (Medis / Multicare / AdvanceCare) — composite 14; lost on weakest PoV alignment (the pilot does not produce the cost-of-care delta insurers need) and slow PT insurer procurement; revisit once BT-03 generates outcome evidence.
